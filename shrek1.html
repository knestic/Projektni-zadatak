Shrek: The Movie

Synopsis

Shrek is a grumpy, solitary ogre who lives in a swamp. When the evil Lord Farquaad decides to take over the swamp, he orders Shrek to rescue the beautiful Princess Fiona from a dragon-guarded castle, in order to win back the deed to his swamp. Shrek sets out on the journey, accompanied by a talking donkey, and eventually falls in love with Fiona.

Cast

  • Mike Myers as Shrek
  • Eddie Murphy as Donkey
  • Cameron Diaz as Princess Fiona
  • John Lithgow as Lord Farquaad

Production

Shrek was produced by DreamWorks Animation and released in 2001. It was directed by Andrew Adamson and Vicky Jenson, and written by Ted Elliott, Terry Rossio, Joe Stillman, and Roger S.H. Schulman. The film was a critical and commercial success, grossing over $484 million at the box office and receiving positive reviews for its animation, voice acting, and humor.

Awards

  • Academy Award for Best Animated Feature
  • Golden Globe Award for Best Animated Feature Film
  • BAFTA Award for Best Children's Film
