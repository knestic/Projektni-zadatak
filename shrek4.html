Shrek the Fourth: The Movie

Synopsis

Shrek and Fiona are now happily married and living in the swamp, but Shrek is feeling a bit bored and restless. When he is offered the chance to become the King of Far, Far Away, he jumps at the opportunity. However, the job is not as easy as he thought it would be, and he starts to question whether he really wants the responsibility. Meanwhile, Fiona's family is causing problems and the kingdom is in danger of being taken over by a group of fairy tale villains.

Cast

  • Mike Myers as Shrek
  • Eddie Murphy as Donkey
  • Cameron Diaz as Princess Fiona
  • Antonio Banderas as Puss in Boots
  • Julie Andrews as Queen Lillian
  • John Cleese as King Harold
  • Rupert Everett as Prince Charming
  • Ian McShane as Rumplestiltskin

Production

Shrek the Fourth was produced by DreamWorks Animation and released in 2010. It was directed by Mike Mitchell and written by Josh Klausner and Darren Lemke. The film received mixed reviews and grossed over $752 million at the box office.

Awards

  • Annie Award for Best Music in an Animated Feature Production
  • Golden Globe Award for Best Animated Feature Film
